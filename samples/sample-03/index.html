Team Onboarding Buddy

A Claude Project that helps new engineers get up to speed using only your team wiki and runbooks — no guessing at your team's real processes.

The Problem

New team members bombard senior engineers with the same questions: "How do I deploy to staging?", "Where are the DB credentials?", "How many approvals does a PR need?" It disrupts flow and slows down onboarding.

This assistant answers those questions from your actual team wiki — not from generic software engineering advice. It knows your exact tools, channels, and processes.

1 Create a Claude Project

  1. Go to claude.ai and sign in.
  2. In the left sidebar, click + New Project.
  3. Name it: Team Onboarding Buddy — Engineering
  4. You'll land on the Project page where you upload docs and set instructions.

Note: Projects require a Claude Pro or Team subscription.

2 Upload These Knowledge Files

In your Project, click Add content → Upload files. Download and upload the file below.

policy.txt
Engineering Team — Wiki & Runbooks ==================================== DEPLOYMENT ---------- Staging deployment: 1. Run: npm run build 2. Run: git push origin staging 3. Staging deploys take approximately 3 minutes to complete. 4. Monitor the deployment in #deploys on Slack. Production deployment: 1. Create a PR from your feature branch to main. 2. Require at least one approval from a senior engineer. 3. After merge, production deploys automatically via CI/CD. 4. Production deploys take approximately 8 minutes. 5. Rollback: run /rollback prod in the #deploys Slack channel. DATABASE ACCESS --------------- Connect using TablePlus (preferred) or psql. Host: db.internal Port: 5432 Credentials: stored in the team 1Password vault under "Dev DB". Ask your team lead for access to the 1Password vault on your first day. Production DB access requires a separate "Prod DB" entry — request from the CTO. ON-CALL ROTATION ---------------- On-call runs Monday–Sunday, one person per week. The on-call schedule is posted in the #on-call Slack channel every Friday. To join the rotation, message @oncall-admin in Slack with your name and preferred weeks. During on-call, respond to PagerDuty alerts within 15 minutes. Escalation: if unresolved in 30 minutes, page the CTO. CODE REVIEW ----------- All PRs require at least one approval from a senior engineer before merging. PRs should be under 400 lines of changed code where possible — break up larger changes. Add a description of what the PR does and why in the PR body. Use the PR template in .github/PULL_REQUEST_TEMPLATE.md. Link the relevant Linear ticket in the PR body. SLACK CHANNELS -------------- #general — company-wide announcements #engineering — engineering team discussion #deploys — deployment notifications and rollbacks #on-call — on-call schedule and alerts #incidents — live incident coordination

3 Paste These System Instructions

In your Project, click Edit project instructions and paste:

You are a Team Onboarding Buddy for the engineering team. You help new team members get up to speed by answering questions using ONLY the team wiki and runbooks provided in your context. Rules you must follow: 1. If the answer is in the wiki, give a clear and helpful answer, citing the relevant section (e.g. "According to the Deployment runbook..."). 2. If the answer is NOT in the wiki, say: "That's not covered in the current team wiki. I'd recommend asking in #engineering on Slack or checking with your team lead." 3. Never invent tooling, processes, or people that are not mentioned in the documents. 4. Be friendly and welcoming — the reader is new to the team. 5. When referencing Slack channels, use the # prefix (e.g. #on-call).

4 Test It

Start a new conversation inside the Project and try these questions:

  • How do I deploy to staging?
  • Where are the database credentials?
  • How do I join the on-call rotation?
  • How many approvals does a PR need?
  • How long does a production deploy take?
  • What channel should I use during a live incident?

Expected quality: Answers should cite the specific wiki section, use your team's real Slack channels (e.g. #deploys, #incidents), and give exact commands. For questions not in the wiki, Claude should point to #engineering on Slack.

Before vs After Demo

Question: "Where are the database credentials?"

❌ Without Context (Claude guessing)

"Database credentials are typically stored in environment variables, a .env file, or a secrets manager like AWS Secrets Manager or HashiCorp Vault. Check with your DevOps team for your specific setup."

Generic — the new hire still has no idea what to actually do on Day 1.

✅ With Context (Grounded answer)

"According to the Database Access section of the team wiki: credentials are stored in the team 1Password vault under 'Dev DB'. Ask your team lead for access to the vault on your first day. For production DB access, you'll need the separate 'Prod DB' entry — request that from the CTO."

Exact steps, real tooling, the new hire knows exactly what to do.
