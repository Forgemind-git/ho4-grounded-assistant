HR Policy Assistant

A Claude Project that answers employee HR questions using only your uploaded policy documents — no hallucinations, no made-up rules.

The Problem

Employees constantly ask HR questions: "How many leave days do I get?", "Do I need a receipt for this expense?", "Can I carry over unused leave?" HR teams spend hours answering the same questions repeatedly.

If you just ask Claude without any context, it gives generic answers based on common HR practices — which may not match your company's specific policies. This assistant solves that by grounding Claude entirely in your actual HR documents.

1 Create a Claude Project

Claude Projects let you upload files and set permanent instructions that apply to every conversation in that project.

  1. Go to claude.ai and sign in with your subscription account.
  2. In the left sidebar, click + New Project.
  3. Name the project: HR Policy Assistant — ACME Corp
  4. You'll land on the Project page — this is where you upload files and set instructions.

Note: Projects are available on Claude Pro and Claude Team plans.

2 Upload These Knowledge Files

In your Project, click Add content → Upload files. Download and upload the files below.

policy.txt
ACME Corp HR Policy — Leave & Expenses ======================================= ANNUAL LEAVE ------------ Employees are entitled to 18 days of paid annual leave per calendar year. Leave must be requested 5 business days in advance via the HR portal. Unused leave can be carried forward up to 5 days into the next calendar year. Leave taken beyond the entitlement will be treated as unpaid leave. SICK LEAVE ---------- Employees are entitled to 10 days of paid sick leave per year. A medical certificate is required for sick leave exceeding 3 consecutive days. Sick leave does not carry forward to the next year. EXPENSE CLAIMS -------------- Expense claims must be submitted within 30 days of the expense date. Receipts are required for all claims over $25. Claims over $500 require manager pre-approval before the expense is incurred. Mileage is reimbursed at $0.65 per kilometre for approved business travel. International travel expenses require VP-level approval regardless of amount. EXPENSE CATEGORIES ------------------ Approved categories: meals, accommodation, transport, office supplies, training materials. Entertainment expenses (client dinners, team events) require a list of attendees. Personal expenses (gym, personal subscriptions) are not reimbursable.

3 Paste These System Instructions

In your Project, click Edit project instructions (or "Set Claude's behavior") and paste the following:

You are an HR Policy Assistant for ACME Corp. You answer questions ONLY using the HR policy documents provided in your context. Rules you must follow: 1. If the answer is in the context, give a clear, specific answer and cite the relevant policy section. 2. If the answer is NOT in the context, say: "I don't have information about that in the current HR policy documents. Please contact HR directly for assistance." 3. Never guess, invent, or use general HR knowledge not present in the provided documents. 4. Keep answers concise and employee-friendly. 5. Always quote or paraphrase the specific policy rule that supports your answer.

Click Save. These instructions apply to every conversation in this project.

4 Test It

Start a new conversation inside the Project and try these questions. Each should produce a grounded, policy-specific answer:

  • How many days annual leave do I get?
  • Can I carry over unused leave?
  • Do I need a receipt for a $20 lunch?
  • How far in advance must I request leave?
  • What happens if I get sick for more than 3 days?
  • Do I need approval before a $600 business expense?

Expected quality: Each answer should cite the specific policy rule (e.g. "Per the Annual Leave section, unused leave can be carried forward up to 5 days…"). If you ask about something not in the policy, Claude should say so and direct you to HR.

Before vs After Demo

Question: "Do I need a receipt for a $20 lunch?"

❌ Without Context (Claude guessing)

"Most companies require receipts for business expenses, though the threshold varies — often around $25–$75. You should check your company's expense policy for the exact cutoff."

Generic, vague, could be wrong for your company.

✅ With Context (Grounded answer)

"No, you don't need a receipt for a $20 lunch. Per the Expense Claims section, receipts are only required for claims over $25. Since $20 is under that threshold, you can submit the claim without a receipt."

Exact, cites the policy, specific to your company.
